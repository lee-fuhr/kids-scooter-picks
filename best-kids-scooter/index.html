Best kids scooters for 2026

Updated June 2026Independently researchedNo paid placement.

Quick answer

The Micro Sprite is our top pick for the best kids scooter in 2026 because it balances durability, smooth ride, and easy control for a wide age range.

The Razor A5 Lux is a great runner-up for older kids who want bigger wheels and a foldable frame, while the Razor A is the budget-friendly choice for occasional use.

A good scooter can be the gateway to hours of outdoor independence for your child. It builds balance, coordination, and confidence, all while being genuinely fun. But not all scooters are created equal: wheel size, weight limit, handlebar height range, and folding mechanisms vary a lot, and picking the wrong one can mean wobbly rides or a scooter your kid outgrows in a season. When choosing, focus on your child’s age and skill level. Three-wheeled scooters are stable for beginners; two-wheeled models suit more confident riders. Look for adjustable handlebars (most scooters cover roughly 2 to 4 years of growth), a low deck for easy pushing, and a reliable brake. Weight limits matter, too, cheaper scooters often max out around 100 pounds, while sturdier ones go higher. And if you plan to toss it in the trunk, pay attention to how easily (or not) it folds.

Best for specific needs

Best for a first scooter

If your child is just learning to balance and push, stability is key. Three-wheeled scooters with a lean-to-steer mechanism (like the Micro Sprite) let kids shift their weight naturally rather than turning the handlebars. A low, wide deck also helps them feel planted. The best overall pick excels here because it balances stability with a smooth ride that encourages confidence. Our pick: Micro Sprite.

Best for older kids who want to ride fast and far

Once kids are past the wobbly stage, they often crave speed and distance. Larger wheels (200 mm or more) roll over bumps with less vibration, and a wider deck gives them room to shift their feet. The runner-up Razor A5 Lux fits this bill perfectly with its big wheels, foldable frame for easy carrying, and lightweight build that doesn’t slow them down. Our pick: Razor A5 Lux.

Best for on-the-go families

A scooter that folds up quickly and compactly can be a game changer for trips to the park, museum, or grocery store. Look for a model with a one-handed folding mechanism and a carrying strap. The compact pick from Globber is designed precisely for this: it collapses into a small package that fits under a stroller seat or in a backpack, and its adjustable handlebar means it grows with your child for several years. Our pick: Globber Go Up.

Our verdict

The right kids scooter comes down to age, riding environment, and how often you plan to use it. For most families, the Micro Sprite is a clear winner, it’s durable, handles well, and lasts for years. If you want something faster for an older kid or need to save money, the Razor A5 Lux and the classic Razor A are proven alternatives. And if you’re always on the move, the Globber Go Up offers unmatched portability without sacrificing safety. Whichever you choose, make sure your child wears a helmet and knee pads, a great scooter is even better when everyone rides safely.
